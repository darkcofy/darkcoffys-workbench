# First 90 Days — Acceleration Plan

> The first 90 days set the trajectory. What you do here determines
> whether you're seen as a "safe hire" or a "future leader."

## Days 1-30: Learn & Map

**Goal:** Understand the landscape. Don't change anything yet. Build trust.

### Week 1-2: Orientation
- [ ] Meet your counselor/mentor — understand promotion criteria explicitly
- [ ] Meet your sponsor/partner — understand what they need from you
- [ ] Map the org chart: who makes decisions, who influences, who blocks
- [ ] Understand the firm's data practice: what services are sold, to whom
- [ ] Get access to all systems (time tracking, project management, knowledge base)
- [ ] Identify the top 3 revenue-generating engagements in your team/practice

### Week 3-4: Team & Client Assessment
- [ ] 1:1 with every direct report — understand their goals, frustrations, strengths
- [ ] 1:1 with every peer manager — understand team dynamics, unwritten rules
- [ ] Read the last 3 engagement post-mortems — what went well, what didn't
- [ ] Identify the team's biggest skills gap
- [ ] Map your client portfolio — who's happy, who's at risk
- [ ] Identify one "quick win" — something visible you can deliver in 30 days

### Day 30 deliverable:
- Team roster filled out (`team/roster.yml`)
- All engagement trackers created (`team/engagements/`)
- Stakeholder map for each client
- First 1:1 notes for every direct report

## Days 31-60: Deliver & Build Credibility

**Goal:** Ship something visible. Start being known for something.

- [ ] Deliver the "quick win" identified in month 1
- [ ] Start weekly status cadence — send to your partner every Friday
- [ ] Identify one process improvement and implement it
  - Examples: standardize dbt project structure, create reusable data quality framework,
    set up CI/CD template, improve estimation process
- [ ] Begin building your "accelerator" — a reusable asset the practice can use
- [ ] Start contributing to BD: join a proposal, help price an engagement, attend a pitch
- [ ] Give your first internal talk/demo (even small — lunch session counts)
- [ ] Have career development conversations with 2 team members
- [ ] Identify your "signature" — the thing you want to be known for
  - Examples: "the dbt person," "the architecture reviewer," "the one who fixes broken projects"

### Day 60 deliverable:
- One completed quick win with visible impact
- Weekly status cadence established
- First BD contribution (even if just reviewing a proposal)
- Promotion tracker started (`team/promotion-tracker.yml`)

## Days 61-90: Lead & Expand

**Goal:** Transition from "learning" to "leading." Start shaping, not just executing.

- [ ] Lead an architecture review or design session (internal or client)
- [ ] Propose a team improvement: new process, tool, training program
- [ ] Start mentoring relationship with 1-2 team members formally
- [ ] Connect with 3 people outside your immediate team (cross-practice networking)
- [ ] Identify your next 2 engagements — be proactive about staffing yourself
- [ ] Write up your first 90-day retrospective — share with your counselor
- [ ] Set quarterly goals aligned to promotion pillars
- [ ] Schedule recurring time for BD activities (protect this in your calendar)

### Day 90 deliverable:
- First quarterly self-assessment filled out in promotion tracker
- Known for at least one thing across the practice
- Active BD pipeline contribution
- Clear plan for next quarter

## Anti-patterns to Avoid

- **Heads-down coder trap** — You're a manager now. If you're writing code 80% of the time, you're not managing. Target: 40% delivery, 30% people, 20% BD, 10% admin.
- **Waiting to be staffed** — Don't wait for work. Go find it. Talk to partners.
- **Ignoring politics** — In consulting, relationships = career. Map the power structure early.
- **Not tracking evidence** — If it's not written down, it didn't happen at review time.
- **Saying yes to everything** — Utilization above 90% means no bandwidth for BD or leadership. Push back.
- **Skipping 1:1s** — Your people are your promotion evidence. Invest in them.

## Weekly Habits

| Day | Action | Time |
|-----|--------|------|
| Monday | Review engagement burn rates, plan the week | 30 min |
| Tuesday | Team standup + 1:1s | 1-2 hours |
| Wednesday | Deep work / client delivery | Full day |
| Thursday | BD activity (proposals, networking, pipeline) | 2 hours |
| Friday | Write weekly status, update engagement trackers, update promotion tracker | 1 hour |
